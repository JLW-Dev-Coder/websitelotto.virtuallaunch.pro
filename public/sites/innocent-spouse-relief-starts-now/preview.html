Common Scenarios

Situations We Understand

You may qualify for innocent spouse relief if any of these situations sound familiar. You are not alone.

Hidden Income

Your spouse or ex-spouse earned income you never knew about and didn't report it on your joint return.

False Deductions

Your partner claimed deductions or credits you had no knowledge of, resulting in an understated tax bill.

Divorce or Separation

You're now divorced or separated, but the IRS is still holding you liable for a joint return you filed together.

Coerced Signing

You were pressured or forced to sign a joint return without understanding what was on it.

Business Fraud

Your spouse ran a business and understated income or inflated expenses — and you had no involvement.

Unfair Tax Burden

Holding you responsible for the full tax debt would be unfair given your circumstances and financial situation.

Eligibility

Do You Qualify?

The IRS offers three forms of relief. Understanding which applies to you is the first step toward protection.

Innocent Spouse Relief

For when your spouse understated tax on a joint return and you had no knowledge or reason to know.

  • Filed a joint return
  • Understated tax due to spouse
  • No knowledge of the error
Most Common

Separation of Liability

Divides the understated tax between you and your spouse. Ideal for divorced or separated individuals.

  • Divorced or legally separated
  • Lived apart for 12+ months
  • No actual knowledge of errors

Equitable Relief

When you don't qualify for the other two but it would be unfair to hold you liable for the tax.

  • Don't qualify for other relief
  • Unfair to hold you liable
  • IRS considers all factors
