Understanding Your Situation

Types of IRS Audits

Not all audits are created equal. Understanding which one you're facing is the first step toward a strong defense.

Correspondence Audit

The most common type. The IRS sends a letter requesting clarification or documentation on specific items in your return.

Low Severity Mail-Based

Office Audit

You're asked to visit an IRS office with specific records. These audits are more in-depth and cover broader areas of your return.

Medium Severity In-Person

Field Audit

The most serious type. An IRS agent visits your home or business to conduct a thorough examination of records and assets.

High Severity On-Site

Time is critical.

IRS audit responses have strict deadlines. Missing them can escalate your case and increase penalties. Act now.
