Trust Structures

Each structure serves a distinct purpose in preserving and transferring wealth across generations.

Revocable Trust

Maintain full control during your lifetime with the flexibility to modify terms, beneficiaries, or dissolve entirely.

  • ◆ Avoids probate
  • ◆ Full amendment rights
  • ◆ Privacy preservation
  • ◆ Incapacity planning

Irrevocable Trust

Remove assets from your taxable estate permanently. Ideal for maximizing generational wealth transfer efficiency.

  • ◆ Estate tax reduction
  • ◆ Asset protection
  • ◆ Creditor shielding
  • ◆ Medicaid planning

Advanced Structures

Dynasty trusts, GRATs, QPRTs, and charitable remainder trusts for sophisticated multi-generational planning.

  • ◆ Dynasty perpetuity
  • ◆ GRAT optimization
  • ◆ Charitable giving
  • ◆ Generation-skipping

Frequently Asked

A revocable trust allows you to maintain control and make changes during your lifetime, while an irrevocable trust permanently removes assets from your estate, providing greater tax benefits and asset protection but less flexibility.

Foundation plans typically complete within 4–6 weeks. Advanced structures may take 8–12 weeks depending on complexity. Legacy tier clients receive ongoing advisory with initial frameworks established within 90 days.

Revocable trusts can be amended at any time. Irrevocable structures have limited modification options but can be designed with flexibility provisions. All Advanced and Legacy tier clients receive annual reviews.

Estates exceeding $5M in combined value typically see the greatest impact from structured planning. However, all estates benefit from proper organization, probate avoidance, and clear beneficiary designations.
