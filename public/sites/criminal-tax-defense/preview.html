Criminal Exposure

Types of charges you may face

Each charge carries severe penalties. Understanding your exposure is the first step toward mounting an effective defense.

26 U.S.C. § 7201

Tax Evasion

Willful attempt to evade or defeat any tax. The most serious tax crime prosecuted by the DOJ.

5 yrs prison $250K fine
26 U.S.C. § 7206

Filing False Returns

Making false declarations under penalties of perjury on any return, statement, or document.

3 yrs prison $250K fine
18 U.S.C. § 371

Tax Conspiracy

Agreement between two or more persons to defraud the United States by impeding the IRS.

5 yrs prison $250K fine
26 U.S.C. § 7203

Failure to File

Willful failure to file returns, pay tax, or supply required information to the IRS.

1 yr prison $100K fine
31 U.S.C. § 5322

FBAR Violations

Willful failure to report foreign bank accounts. Increasingly prosecuted with offshore investigations.

10 yrs prison $500K fine
18 U.S.C. § 1956

Money Laundering

Financial transactions designed to conceal proceeds. Often charged alongside tax evasion.

20 yrs prison $500K fine
